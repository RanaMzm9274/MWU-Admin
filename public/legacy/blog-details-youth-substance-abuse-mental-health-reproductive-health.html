Madda Walabu University organized a discussion forum focused on youth substance abuse, mental health, and reproductive health issues. The forum was aimed at addressing challenges facing young people through collaborative efforts and fostering healthy and productive citizens.

The forum brought together participants from various sectors, including offices responsible for women and children affairs, sports and youth affairs, security and public safety, education offices and schools, university students, and other relevant stakeholders.

Opening Session

During the opening session, Dr. Bezabih Wondimu, Vice President for Academic Affairs, Research, Technology Transfer, and Community Service at Madda Walabu University, welcomed participants and delivered the keynote address. He emphasized the crucial role of youth in national development and highlighted the University's ongoing project designed to promote youth health and well-being, enabling them to become productive members of society.

Dr. Bezabih further noted that the magnitude of these challenges requires a coordinated response and stressed the importance of strengthening collaboration among all stakeholders to effectively address the issues.

Stakeholder Participation

  • Offices responsible for women and children affairs.
  • Sports and youth affairs offices.
  • Security and public safety offices.
  • Education offices and schools.
  • University students and invited stakeholders.

The diversity of participants created a collaborative environment for sharing ideas and strengthening community-level engagement around youth health and well-being.

Project Overview

Dr. Teshome Sirak, Head of the President’s Office at Madda Walabu University, provided an overview of the activities undertaken under the project so far and underscored the necessity of continued joint efforts and coordinated action moving forward.

Speaking on behalf of the University's Technology Transfer and Community Service Directorate, Dr. Mohammed Jemal explained that, in line with directives from the Ministry of Education, the University's community service initiatives are currently focused on four major areas.

  • Support for schools.
  • Prevention of illegal migration.
  • Reduction of traffic accidents.
  • Addressing youth substance abuse, mental health, and reproductive health issues.

Discussion Paper and Technical Presentation

The project's principal investigator, Dr. Adam Ismail, who also serves as Managing Director of the Referral Hospital at the Goba College of Medicine and Health Sciences, presented the discussion paper that served as the basis for the forum. He explained that the current phase of the project will be implemented in four schools and two university campuses.

He also provided a comprehensive overview of the severity and complexity of the issues being addressed. In addition, Assistant Professor Genet Fikadu delivered a detailed presentation on the challenges surrounding youth reproductive health and outlined potential future directions and interventions.

Coordinated effort, partnership, and sustained collaboration are essential to address youth substance abuse, mental health, and reproductive health challenges effectively.

Forum Key Message

Participant Reflections

At the conclusion of the forum, participants shared numerous constructive ideas and recommendations. They collectively agreed that the challenges facing youth can only be effectively addressed through coordinated efforts, partnership, and sustained collaboration among all concerned stakeholders.

The discussion also reinforced the importance of continued health education, community involvement, and institutional coordination in building healthy and productive young citizens.

Key Takeaways

  • Youth health challenges require coordinated institutional and community action.
  • Substance abuse, mental health, and reproductive health should be addressed together.
  • Schools and universities are important platforms for prevention and awareness.
  • Stakeholder collaboration improves the reach and impact of community service projects.

Source: Madda Walabu University Public and International Relations Office.
