Admission Process of Madda Walabu University

The admission process of Madda Walabu University is governed by the Madda Walabu University Senate Legislation 2024 and implemented through the Senate, Registrar, Academic Programs Directorate, and Admissions and Placement Committee (APC). Admission covers regular programs, Continuing Education Programs, distance education, advanced standing admissions, and graduate programs.

1. Institutional Responsibility for Admission

The University Registrar is responsible for student admission, placement, registration, and academic records management. The Senate determines academic standards, approves academic programs, regulates academic affairs, and supervises quality education implementation. The Admissions and Placement Committee (APC) administers admissions and placement procedures.

2. Announcement of Admission

The University announces available programs and admission opportunities for regular programs, Continuing Education Programs (CEP), distance education, advanced standing admissions, and graduate programs.

  • Programs are offered through colleges, schools, institutes, and departments.
  • Applicants should follow official announcements for intake and eligibility information.
  • Admission opportunities may vary by academic unit and program.

3. Submission of Applications

Applicants submit educational certificates and transcripts, national examination results, identification documents, completed application forms, and additional documents required by departments. International students also submit valid passport or ID documents and residence or refugee documentation where applicable.

4. Verification of Credentials

The Registrar Office verifies the authenticity of documents, academic qualifications, entrance eligibility, and compliance with Ministry of Education requirements. Academic units evaluate secondary and post-secondary credentials pursued elsewhere.

5. Admission Screening and Placement

The APC oversees the implementation of admissions and placements, determines admission procedures, and places students into programs based on available capacity and eligibility criteria. It also determines regular admissions, private admissions, advanced standing admissions, and CEP admissions.

6. Special Admission Consideration

Special admissions may be granted considering gender equity, disability, educational disadvantage, and other affirmative action considerations approved by academic units and the APC. No special admission criteria may be implemented without prior APC approval.

7. Admission Decision and Registration

After evaluation, qualified applicants are approved, placement decisions are finalized, and admission letters are issued by the Registrar. Admitted students then complete semester registration, course registration, ID card processing, and academic orientation. Graduate students must also complete enrollment and registration according to Graduate Program regulations.
