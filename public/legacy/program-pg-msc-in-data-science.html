Graduate Degree

MSc in Data Science

The program prepares graduates for data analytics, modeling, software support, and evidence-driven decision making.

Program Overview

The curriculum develops statistical thinking, data handling, programming, and applied data science skills.

Duration and Course Structure

  • The program combines data science theory, programming, statistics, and project-based learning.
  • Students study data management, machine learning foundations, analytics, and visualization concepts.
  • Applied lab or project work strengthens practical competence.

Thesis and Final Requirements

  • A thesis or major project is required for completion.
  • Students must complete all course and assessment requirements.
  • Final approval follows academic clearance and institutional review.

Admission and Student Readiness

Applicants are expected to meet MWU graduate admission rules, hold an appropriate first degree or equivalent preparation, and satisfy department screening, entrance, or professional requirements where applicable. Students should review the curriculum PDF for prerequisite background, semester sequencing, and department-specific rules before applying or registering.

Learning Outcomes

  • Build strong competence in technical problem-solving, design, computing, systems thinking, laboratory or studio practice, and applied innovation.
  • Apply classroom knowledge through assignments, laboratory, practicum, studio, field, clinical, or research activities where required by the curriculum.
  • Use ethical reasoning, teamwork, communication, and problem-solving skills in professional and community contexts.

Assessment and Academic Progress

Assessment includes coursework, seminars, assignments, practical work where relevant, thesis or project supervision, and final approval by the department and graduate program procedures. Students are expected to satisfy attendance, academic standing, and graduation clearance requirements set by the university.

Career Pathways and Further Study

Graduates may pursue roles in engineering practice, software and systems work, infrastructure development, technical operations, consulting, research, and public-sector projects. The program also supports further study, research, professional certification, or specialization depending on the graduate's field and performance.
