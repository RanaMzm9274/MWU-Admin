Access to Justice

Legal assistance, education, and advocacy for underserved communities

MWU legal aid services support people who need access to legal guidance, rights-based advocacy, and community legal education. The service combines professional oversight, student learning, and ethical support for vulnerable groups.

Civil Rights and Gender Protection

  • Legal support for gender-based violence cases.
  • Women's rights advocacy and representation.
  • Workplace discrimination and harassment support.
  • Equal pay and employment rights guidance.

Family Law Services

  • Marriage and divorce proceeding support.
  • Child custody and support matters.
  • Domestic violence protection guidance.
  • Case referral and follow-up assistance.

Legal Education

  • Legal rights workshops and seminars.
  • Community legal literacy programmes.
  • Youth legal awareness initiatives.
  • Free advice and legal consultation clinics.
Service Process

How cases are supported

1

Case Intake

Initial consultation and assessment of the legal issue.

2

Legal Analysis

Review available evidence, rights, risks, and legal options.

3

Representation

Provide legal support, referral, or representation as appropriate.

4

Follow-up

Monitor progress and continue support after initial action.
