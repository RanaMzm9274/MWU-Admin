Madda Walabu University Celebrates Its 18th Graduation Ceremony

Madda Walabu University proudly celebrated its 18th Graduation Ceremony on June 27, 2026, awarding Bachelor's and Master's degrees to 1,089 graduates from its various colleges.

University President Dr. Berhanemeskel Tena congratulated the graduates and encouraged them to uphold professionalism, integrity, compassion, and service in a world being reshaped by AI and digital technologies.

MWU Hosts 2nd International Conference on Tourism and Hospitality Education through AI

Madda Walabu University successfully hosted its 2nd International Conference on Tourism and Hospitality Education through Artificial Intelligence, convening scholars, policymakers, researchers, and industry experts from Ethiopia and abroad.

The program also featured the official launch of MWU's new website and AI Consultation chatbot developed and integrated by Micro Tech London for student consultation and convenience.

29Apr

MWU and BOSAD Hold Awareness Dialogue with Traditional Bone Setters

The College of Medicine and Health Sciences at Madda Walabu University, in partnership with the national BOSAD research team, held a one-day awareness and capacity-building dialogue at Goba Referral Hospital.

More than 50 traditional practitioners from Robe Town, Goba, Dinsho, Agarfa, and Sinana participated, focusing on safer fracture care, timely referral, and collaboration with modern health services.

MWU Hosts STI Policy, Digital Ethiopia 2030 and Startup Proclamation Workshop

Madda Walabu University, in collaboration with the Ministry of Innovation and Technology, held a policy and innovation workshop focused on Ethiopia's National STI Policy, the Digital Ethiopia 2030 Strategy, and the Startup Proclamation.

The programme recognised student innovators, strengthened startup readiness, and promoted coordinated support among universities, government, and partners for innovation-driven national development.

MWU Announces First-Ever Special Presidential Award Trophy Winner

In the history of Madda Walabu University, the first-ever Special Presidential Award Trophy was awarded to a graduating student who achieved the highest overall score among all graduates.

The award recognises outstanding academic distinction and sets a new benchmark of excellence for future graduating classes.
