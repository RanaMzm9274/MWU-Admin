Graduate Degree

MSc in Public Health in Epidemiology

The program prepares public health professionals with advanced epidemiology and biostatistics skills for research, teaching, management, and evidence-based intervention design.

Program Overview

The curriculum states that the program is designed to expand postgraduate training in Ethiopia, strengthen capacity within MWU and the nation, and produce graduates able to analyze, synthesize, and interpret health and community data.

Admission Requirements

  • First degree in health or health-related fields such as Health Officer, Nursing, Pharmacy, Medical Laboratory Sciences, Medicine, Midwifery, Environmental Health, Demography, Health Informatics, Biology, or Sociology.
  • Minimum first-degree CGPA of 2.0 or above.
  • At least two years of work experience after the bachelor’s degree is preferred.
  • Three letters of recommendation from instructors, employers, or professional associations.
  • English language test may be required where English was not the medium of instruction.
  • Preliminary screening followed by written and/or oral entrance examination.

Graduate Profile

  • Conduct critical analysis in leadership, public health research, and evidence-based practice.
  • Perform advanced data analysis using statistical software.
  • Design valid epidemiologic studies and appraise public health literature critically.
  • Apply health evaluation methods and develop surveillance and disease control approaches.
  • Teach, conduct research, provide consultancy, and support health policy implementation.

Duration and Course Structure

  • The program runs for three years across six semesters.
  • Students complete 39 credit hours (63 ECTS), including independent thesis work.
  • Training emphasizes epidemiology, biostatistics, data analysis, public health research, and evidence-based intervention design.

Thesis and Final Requirements

  • A thesis is part of the graduation requirement.
  • Students must achieve a cumulative GPA of 3.00 or above.
  • A minimum grade of B- is required at graduation, with no C grade allowed.
  • The final award is approved through the Graduate Studies and Registrar channels.

Admission and Student Readiness

Applicants are expected to meet MWU graduate admission rules, hold an appropriate first degree or equivalent preparation, and satisfy department screening, entrance, or professional requirements where applicable. Students should review the curriculum PDF for prerequisite background, semester sequencing, and department-specific rules before applying or registering.

Learning Outcomes

  • Build strong competence in patient care, public health, biomedical science, clinical reasoning, professional ethics, and evidence-based practice.
  • Apply classroom knowledge through assignments, laboratory, practicum, studio, field, clinical, or research activities where required by the curriculum.
  • Use ethical reasoning, teamwork, communication, and problem-solving skills in professional and community contexts.

Assessment and Academic Progress

Assessment includes coursework, seminars, assignments, practical work where relevant, thesis or project supervision, and final approval by the department and graduate program procedures. Students are expected to satisfy attendance, academic standing, and graduation clearance requirements set by the university.

Career Pathways and Further Study

Graduates may pursue roles in health service delivery, hospitals, public health offices, laboratories, NGOs, clinical teams, teaching, and health research. The program also supports further study, research, professional certification, or specialization depending on the graduate's field and performance.
