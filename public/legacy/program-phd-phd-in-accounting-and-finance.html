PhD Program

PhD in Accounting and Finance

This program is part of Madda Walabu University's academic portfolio aligned with quality education, employability, research, and societal problem-solving.

Program Overview

PhD in Accounting and Finance is offered by Madda Walabu University as part of its academic mission in teaching, research, community service, and professional preparation.

Duration and Course Structure

PhD study normally combines advanced seminars, comprehensive or qualifying work, proposal defense, supervised dissertation research, and final public defense according to university graduate rules. The curriculum document provides the detailed course list, credit structure, semester flow, and department requirements.

Thesis, Project, or Dissertation

Students complete the required doctoral coursework and seminars, pass required evaluations, prepare and defend a research proposal, complete supervised dissertation research, and defend the dissertation successfully.

Admission and Student Readiness

Applicants are expected to meet MWU graduate admission rules, hold the required prior degree in a related field, satisfy department screening requirements, and present research readiness for doctoral work. Students should review the curriculum PDF for prerequisite background, semester sequencing, and department-specific rules before applying or registering.

Learning Outcomes

  • Build strong competence in business analysis, responsible decision-making, financial literacy, organizational performance, and professional communication.
  • Apply classroom knowledge through assignments, laboratory, practicum, studio, field, clinical, or research activities where required by the curriculum.
  • Use ethical reasoning, teamwork, communication, and problem-solving skills in professional and community contexts.

Assessment and Academic Progress

Assessment includes advanced coursework where required, seminar participation, comprehensive or qualifying evaluation, dissertation progress review, publication or presentation expectations where applicable, and final defense. Students are expected to satisfy attendance, academic standing, and graduation clearance requirements set by the university.

Career Pathways and Further Study

Graduates may pursue roles in accounting, finance, management, entrepreneurship, consulting, public administration, banking, project coordination, and business research. The program also supports further study, research, professional certification, or specialization depending on the graduate's field and performance.
