Madda Walabu University Medical and Health Science College of Goba Referral Hospital successfully organized a comprehensive awareness creation and panel discussion program focused on cervical cancer prevention, early detection, and treatment.

The event brought together stakeholders from various organizations, health professionals, the Education Office, the Women and Children Bureau, community representatives, and invited guests to strengthen public awareness and encourage collective action in the fight against cervical cancer.

Program Overview

The program was designed to create practical awareness about cervical cancer and to support a shared response from health institutions, community leaders, and public offices. It highlighted the importance of prevention, early detection, and timely treatment as the most effective ways to reduce the burden of the disease.

  • Focused on cervical cancer prevention, early detection, and treatment.
  • Brought together health professionals, public offices, and community representatives.
  • Promoted coordinated public awareness and shared responsibility.
  • Encouraged regular screening and timely treatment.

Opening Remarks

The program officially commenced with a welcoming and opening speech delivered by Associate Professor Mohammedaman Mama, Chief Executive Director of Medical and Health Science College of Goba Referral Hospital. In his remarks, he emphasized the critical importance of cervical cancer awareness and the need for coordinated effort among healthcare institutions, stakeholders, and the community.

He further noted that awareness creation, regular screening, and timely treatment are essential measures in preventing cervical cancer and saving lives. He also reaffirmed the hospital’s commitment to expanding cancer-related health services and community education programs.

Research Presentation

During the program, the Director of the Cancer Center at Medical and Health Science College of Goba Referral Hospital, Dr. Abubeker Kedir, presented research findings related to cervical cancer. His presentation addressed the current status of cervical cancer, major risk factors, prevention mechanisms, and the importance of early screening and diagnosis.

He also stressed the growing need for continuous public education and stronger healthcare interventions to address cervical cancer effectively within the community. The presentation created a strong basis for productive discussion among participants.

Awareness creation, regular screening, and timely treatment are essential measures in preventing cervical cancer and saving lives.

Associate Professor Mohammedaman Mama

Participant Response

Participants highly appreciated Madda Walabu University Medical and Health Science College of Goba Referral Hospital for organizing such an important and timely event. They noted that the panel discussion created a valuable opportunity to gain knowledge and exchange ideas regarding cervical cancer prevention and control.

They also expressed readiness to disseminate the knowledge and awareness gained from the program to their respective communities and institutions, contributing to broader public awareness and improved community health outcomes.

Key Takeaways

  • Cervical cancer can be reduced through awareness, screening, and timely treatment.
  • Cross-sector collaboration strengthens prevention and community education.
  • Health institutions play a central role in public education and service expansion.
  • Community representatives can extend awareness to wider audiences.

The awareness creation and panel discussion program concluded with a strong call for continued collaboration among stakeholders, healthcare professionals, and the community in the ongoing efforts to combat cervical cancer and promote public health.
