About MWU

A Public University Driving Societal Impact in Ethiopia

Madda Walabu University currently enrolls around 24,961 students in undergraduate and postgraduate studies through 79 departments under seven colleges and three schools. MWU is committed to producing competent, entrepreneurial, and employable graduates through relevant and quality education, research, training, consultancy, and community service.

History of Walabu

The Historical Roots of Madda Walabu University

Madda Walabu University is named after the historical place called Madda Walabu, known in Oromo oral traditions as the cradle land of the Oromo people and the birthplace of the Gadaa system.

Madda Walabu is associated with long-standing cultural, spiritual, and socio-political assemblies. The area is located in the southeastern Ethiopian highlands near the upper Genale River and remains a powerful heritage symbol for Oromo identity and leadership.

The foundation stone of Madda Walabu University was laid in February 2005, and the university officially began in December 2007. Today, the university carries this legacy forward through quality education, research, and community-centered transformation.
