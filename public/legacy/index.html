About Madda Walabu University

A Leading Public University Advancing Ethiopia Through Education and Innovation

Madda Walabu University currently serves around 16,320 students in undergraduate and postgraduate programs through 79 departments under seven colleges and three schools. The university aligns education and research with real societal needs to drive national development.

Seven Colleges and Three Schools

Academic units include Business and Economics, Social Sciences and Humanities, Natural and Computational Sciences, Agriculture and Natural Resources, Education and Behavioral Sciences, Engineering, and Computing, plus Schools of Health Sciences, Medicine, and Law.

Explore key facts about Madda Walabu University, including campuses, academics, admissions, research priorities, and our 2030 strategic direction.

MWU is recognized for quality education, community engagement, and practical research with a strategic goal to become one of Ethiopia's top five societal problem-solving universities by 2030.

The main campus is in Bale Robe, Oromia Regional State, with additional campuses in Goba and Shashemene, enabling broader access to higher education.

MWU serves around 16,320 students through 79 departments in undergraduate and postgraduate programs delivered across diverse fields and professions.

Vision: to become one of the top five societal problem-solving universities in Ethiopia by 2030. Mission: to produce competent graduates and deliver need-based community service, training, consultancy, and research outputs.

Beyond three campuses, MWU has integrated research centers at Robe Main Campus, Bidire Town in Madda Walabu Woreda, and Harawa 1 in Ginir Woreda.

Prospective students, researchers, development partners, and institutions can connect with MWU via the admissions and contact channels to explore study, research, and collaboration opportunities.
