Admission Criteria of Madda Walabu University

Admission criteria differ by program level, applicant background, and delivery mode. MWU reviews each applicant against national higher education requirements, university Senate legislation, program capacity, department rules, academic records, and document authenticity. Graduate admission is governed under Article 107 of the Senate Legislation.

Undergraduate Admission Criteria

Applicants seeking admission into undergraduate programs must meet national educational requirements, program-specific requirements, and document verification rules.

  • Complete preparatory education or the nationally accepted equivalent.
  • Fulfill Ministry of Education entrance requirements and national placement standards.
  • Meet subject background and academic readiness requirements for the selected program.
  • Submit complete and verifiable educational and identification documents.
  • Undergraduate departments may also set additional subject requirements, entrance examinations, or interviews where necessary.

Continuing Education Program Admission

CEP applicants may be admitted based on educational qualification, work experience, program relevance, and institutional requirements. CEP includes evening, summer in-service, distance education, online, and correspondence programs.

  • Educational qualification appropriate to the program level.
  • Relevant work experience where required by the program or department.
  • Ability to attend the approved delivery mode and academic calendar.
  • Fulfillment of registration, payment, and document verification requirements where applicable.
  • Department approval when professional background or prerequisite knowledge is required.

Advanced Standing Admission

Advanced standing applicants may receive admission based on prior diploma or degree qualifications, transferable academic credits, and equivalence evaluations by academic units.

  • Recognized diploma, degree, or equivalent qualification.
  • Official transcripts showing completed courses, grades, credit hours, and academic standing.
  • Course equivalence review by the relevant department or academic unit.
  • Transferable credits that match MWU curriculum requirements.
  • Admission decision based on eligibility, space, curriculum compatibility, and APC approval.

International Student Admission

International students must meet equivalent academic standards, submit legal residency or refugee documentation where applicable, and fulfill university and national regulations for international admissions.

  • Academic qualifications equivalent to the required Ethiopian entry level.
  • Valid passport or accepted identification document.
  • Residence, visa, refugee, or sponsorship documentation where applicable.
  • Official transcripts, certificates, and certified translations where required.
  • Compliance with national regulations for international study.

Special Admission Criteria

The University may provide affirmative consideration for female applicants, students with disabilities, and educationally disadvantaged groups.

  • Evidence supporting the special admission category where required.
  • Review by the relevant academic unit and admissions body.
  • Compliance with the minimum academic requirement for the program.
  • Approval before any special criterion is applied.
  • Fairness, transparency, and equal opportunity principles in selection.

Master's Program Admission

Master's applicants must hold a recognized bachelor's degree, meet departmental CGPA requirements, satisfy departmental entrance requirements, and submit official transcripts and credentials.

  • Recognized bachelor's degree or equivalent qualification in a relevant field.
  • Official transcript and degree certificate from the awarding institution.
  • Departmental CGPA and prerequisite requirements.
  • Entrance examination, interview, or concept note where required.
  • Recommendation letters or work experience evidence where the program requires them.

PhD Program Admission

PhD applicants must hold a recognized master's degree, demonstrate research capability, present research interests or proposals where required, and meet departmental and university standards.

  • Recognized master's degree or equivalent qualification in a relevant field.
  • Strong academic record and evidence of research readiness.
  • Research concept note, proposal, or statement of research interest where required.
  • Departmental and university approval based on academic standards.
  • Entrance examination or interview where required by the program.

Departmental Review

Graduate applications are reviewed by departments, graduate committees, and relevant academic units before approval.

  • All applications are subject to verification and institutional approval.
  • Applicants should follow the current official admission announcement for deadlines and program-specific updates.
  • Admission may be limited by academic standards and program capacity.
