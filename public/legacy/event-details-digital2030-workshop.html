About the event

Madda Walabu University held a workshop on Ethiopia's National Science, Technology and Innovation (STI) Policy, the Digital Ethiopia 2030 Strategy, and the Startup Proclamation in collaboration with the Ministry of Innovation and Technology.

The forum highlighted a central message from H.E. Dr Belete Molla: "Innovation- and technology-enriched human capital is not only essential but an obligation for our country's comprehensive advancement."

  • Creating shared understanding of the STI Policy, Digital Ethiopia 2030, and the Startup Proclamation.
  • Identifying, training, and formally recognising students and youth with strong innovation potential.
  • Encouraging role-model impact so more young people engage in practical innovation and problem-solving.
  • Strengthening joint action between universities, ministry institutions, and partners to support startup ecosystems.

In his welcome remarks, MWU President Dr Birhanemeskel Tena stated that the workshop is a clear product of collaboration with the Ministry and a platform to deepen national innovation capacity. He emphasised that Ethiopia needs a generation that is inquisitive, critical, and creative, and confirmed that MWU has already launched implementation of its long-term innovation roadmap.

In his opening speech, Dr Belete Molla, Minister of Innovation and Technology and Chair of the MWU Board, explained that such workshops have two main aims: to identify and recognise talented innovators through structured training, and to inspire wider youth participation through positive examples. He noted that, this year, the Ministry is working with two universities and that MWU is one of them.

In the closing session, Dr Bayissa Bedada, State Minister responsible for the Innovation and Research sector, called on MWU and all stakeholders to continue encouraging young innovators and to work in close partnership, reaffirming that the Ministry will remain an active supporter.

During the seven-day programme period, 65 competitors from Bale, East Bale, and West Arsi zones presented innovation concept plans. Based on the Ministry's assessment criteria, 35 were selected for training, and recognition certificates were awarded to the top 10 winners by senior officials including the Minister, the MWU President, State Minister, and MWU Vice Presidents.
