Specialty in Gynecology and Obstetrics

The program is designed to produce obstetricians and gynecologists who can reduce maternal and perinatal morbidity and mortality and deliver high-quality reproductive health care.

Program Overview

The curriculum is built around clinical training, research, community service, and service delivery in obstetrics and gynecology.

Admission Requirements

  • Registered medical practitioner licensed to practice in Ethiopia.
  • Appropriate administrative approval and placement into the residency program.
  • Capacity to complete mandatory and elective rotations and clinical experiences.
  • Readiness for collaborative work in a teaching hospital setting.

Graduate Profile

  • Provides comprehensive obstetric and gynecologic clinical care.
  • Identifies social, economic, environmental, biological, and emotional determinants of maternal health.
  • Applies reproductive health principles, ethics, teaching, research, and leadership skills.
  • Works as a team member and demonstrates professionalism and nationalism.

Duration and Course Structure

  • Training is organized around mandatory and elective rotations in obstetrics and gynecology.
  • Residents complete supervised clinical work, service delivery, teaching, and research activities in the department and affiliated hospitals.
  • The program depends on qualified faculty, adequate patient volume, and supporting clinical and diagnostic services.

Thesis and Rotation Requirements

  • Residents must complete the full set of planned clinical rotations and attachments.
  • Learning is strengthened through supervised exposure to a wide variety of maternal, fetal, and surgical cases.
  • Research, clinical documentation, and professional conduct are expected throughout training.

Admission and Student Readiness

Applicants are expected to meet MWU graduate admission rules, hold an appropriate first degree or equivalent preparation, and satisfy department screening, entrance, or professional requirements where applicable. Students should review the curriculum PDF for prerequisite background, semester sequencing, and department-specific rules before applying or registering.

Learning Outcomes

  • Build strong competence in patient care, public health, biomedical science, clinical reasoning, professional ethics, and evidence-based practice.
  • Apply classroom knowledge through assignments, laboratory, practicum, studio, field, clinical, or research activities where required by the curriculum.
  • Use ethical reasoning, teamwork, communication, and problem-solving skills in professional and community contexts.

Assessment and Academic Progress

Assessment includes coursework, seminars, assignments, practical work where relevant, thesis or project supervision, and final approval by the department and graduate program procedures. Students are expected to satisfy attendance, academic standing, and graduation clearance requirements set by the university.

Career Pathways and Further Study

Graduates may pursue roles in health service delivery, hospitals, public health offices, laboratories, NGOs, clinical teams, teaching, and health research. The program also supports further study, research, professional certification, or specialization depending on the graduate's field and performance.
