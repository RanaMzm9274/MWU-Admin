About the event

Madda Walabu University successfully hosted its 2nd International Conference on Tourism and Hospitality Education through Artificial Intelligence (AI) on June 22, 2026. The conference brought together scholars, policymakers, researchers, industry experts, and institutional partners from Ethiopia and abroad to examine how intelligent technologies can strengthen tourism education, hospitality training, destination management, and service innovation.

The event created a focused academic and professional forum for discussing the future of tourism and hospitality in an increasingly digital world. Participants explored how AI can support curriculum modernization, improve visitor experience, expand data-informed destination planning, and help universities prepare graduates for technology-enabled tourism and hospitality industries.

  • Advanced dialogue on AI-driven tourism and hospitality education.
  • Connected university researchers with policymakers, industry leaders, and international scholars.
  • Highlighted sustainable destination development and digital innovation as priority themes.
  • Supported national conversations on education, innovation, tourism competitiveness, and technology adoption.

In his welcome address, University President Dr. Berhanemeskel Tena underlined the growing importance of tourism and hospitality education in national development. He noted that the sector is closely tied to employment creation, cultural exchange, local economic growth, and Ethiopia's broader development ambitions. He also emphasized that universities have a responsibility to equip students with practical, innovative, and globally relevant skills.

During the opening ceremony, Dr. Berhanemeskel Tena officially launched Madda Walabu University's newly developed website. He expressed appreciation to the website development team and extended gratitude to the Embassies of the United Kingdom and Indonesia for their valuable support in strengthening the university's digital presence and international collaboration.

Digital Transformation Milestone

MWU Website Officially Launched

The new Madda Walabu University website, developed by Micro Tech London, was formally launched in the presence of ministers, government officials, university leadership, international partners, scholars, and conference participants. The launch marked an important step in presenting MWU's academic programs, research work, admissions information, events, partnerships, and public services through a modern digital platform.

The platform strengthens the university's visibility, improves access to information for students and partners, and supports MWU's wider commitment to innovation, transparency, and service-oriented communication. As part of this digital upgrade, Micro Tech London also developed and integrated the AI Consultation chatbot to support student consultation and improve everyday convenience for students seeking quick guidance.

  • Modern university digital presence
  • Clearer access to programs and admissions
  • AI Consultation chatbot for students
  • Improved news, events, and public updates
Technology Partner

Recognition for Micro Tech London and CEO Shehzad Saleem

Madda Walabu University recognizes Micro Tech London for its professional contribution to the University's new website and AI Consultation chatbot. CEO Shehzad Saleem provided focused leadership, technical direction, and a strong commitment to quality throughout the development process. His work with the university team helped deliver a polished, accessible, and future-ready platform that reflects MWU's academic identity and supports its public engagement goals.

Student Convenience

AI Consultation Chatbot for Student Support

The newly integrated AI Consultation chatbot gives students a convenient digital support channel for common consultation needs. Developed and integrated by Micro Tech London, the chatbot helps students access guidance more quickly, ask questions about university services, and receive practical direction without waiting for manual support. This feature strengthens MWU's student-centered digital services and makes the new website more useful for day-to-day student engagement.

The conference was officially opened by Dr. Belete Molla, Minister of Innovation and Technology and Chairperson of the University Board. In his remarks, he emphasized the transformative role of artificial intelligence in education, innovation, and the tourism sector. His message focused on the need for institutions to embrace emerging technologies and prepare learners, researchers, and industries for a rapidly changing digital economy.

Keynote addresses were delivered by senior government officials and renowned international scholars. Their presentations focused on AI-driven tourism, sustainable destination development, digital innovation, and the role of higher education in building resilient tourism and hospitality systems. The sessions encouraged participants to consider both the opportunities and responsibilities that come with adopting AI in service-oriented sectors.

The conference attracted participants from Ethiopia and several countries across Europe, South America, and Africa. With both in-person and virtual participation, the event expanded MWU's international academic network and reinforced the university's commitment to research, digital transformation, and meaningful community and industry engagement.
